Slides

Slides are versatile and effective, when used properly. However, your average run-of-the-mill presentations are anti-eLearning and not suitable for presenter-less or internet-based application. What I am attempting to show in this example, is that slides can be used to create short animated videos appropriate for briefly illustrating a point.

Another error I often see is estimating the size of a presentation (or eLearning course) by the amount of slides a deck contains; the real indicator of length is how many words there are in the script.

Screencasts

In creating screencasts, it is best to always make Closed Captions available to the viewer and make sure that the script would be coherent even if the viewer was unable to see the illutrations: Videos that say things like "click here" are annoying to someone listening to only the audio. It is better to say, for example, "click the login button at the top right of the screen."
